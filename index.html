who i am

I want to work on products that earn their place — that genuinely improve people's relationship with something difficult. Financial services, insurance, pensions; sectors where the gap between what exists and what people actually need tends to be large and where getting it right takes more than good intentions.

I'm looking for organisations that take that seriously. Companies using technology to make something genuinely better for people who've been underserved, overlooked or badly served by the status quo. Places where product leadership means having real accountability for outcomes — not just managing delivery.

what i do

I define product vision and strategy across portfolios, turning complex problem spaces into clear priorities and outcome-led roadmaps. I work closely with engineering, design and commercial leaders to shape how products are built as well as what is built. This often involves improving operating models, clarifying ownership and helping teams focus on the decisions that matter most.

My approach balances customer insight, technical understanding and commercial pragmatism. I’m comfortable engaging in platform architecture and delivery trade-offs while keeping the conversation grounded in customer value and business impact.

why i do it

I'm motivated as much by improving how organisations work as by what they ship. The interesting problems are usually structural - unclear ownership, misaligned incentives, teams that are capable but stuck. Untangling that, and building the conditions for good product thinking to take hold, is what I find genuinely satisfying.

I also care about who ends up on the other side of what gets built. The most interesting work I've done has been in sectors where people are genuinely underserved — where a better product doesn't just perform well commercially, it actually changes something for someone. That's the kind of impact I want to keep working towards.
